INTERNATIONAL FORUM “EDUCATION, SCIENCE, INNOVATION: HUMAN CAPITAL IN THE POST-WAR RECONSTRUCTION OF UKRAINE”

December 11–12, 2025

Dragomanov Ukrainian State University, Kyiv

Working languages: Ukrainian, English (with simultaneous interpretation).

Plenary Session

Mykhailo Zgurovsky, Academician of the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine, Academician of the National Academy of Educational Sciences of Ukraine, Chairman of the Ukrainian Peace Council
“Innovative development of Ukraine – the only path to post-war recovery and national competitiveness”

Anatoliy Zahorodniy, Academician of the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine, President of the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine
“Science as a driving force of post-war recovery and innovation”

Vasyl Kremen, Academician of the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine, Academician of the National Academy of Educational Sciences of Ukraine, President of the National Academy of Educational Sciences of Ukraine
“The human being amid modern civilizational and national challenges”

Viktor Andrushchenko, Corresponding Member of the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine, Academician of the National Academy of Educational Sciences of Ukraine, Rector of Mykhailo Drahomanov Ukrainian State University
“Training modern teachers as a key condition for education modernization”

Ella Libanova, Academician of the National Academy of Educational Sciences of Ukraine
“Human capital formation: challenges of war and post-war recovery”

Volodymyr Bugrov, Rector of Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv
“International cooperation in education as an essential factor of modernization of national education”

One or two presentations by international participants (to be confirmed)

Key Panels

1. Strategic directions and factors of human capital formation

  • Educational and scientific priorities for Ukraine’s socio-economic recovery
  • New forms of learning and research in the context of digital transformation
  • Artificial intelligence in science and education
Main organizer: National Academy of Educational Sciences of Ukraine.
Venue: Dragomanov Ukrainian State University.

2. Science as a driver of post-war recovery and innovation

  • The role of fundamental research in creating new technologies and materials
  • State support and international cooperation in fundamental science
  • Challenges and opportunities for the development of fundamental sciences in the context of national reconstruction
Main organizer: National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine.
Venue: Dragomanov Ukrainian State University.

3. Innovations in Ukraine’s post-war recovery

  • Developing innovation infrastructure in universities: legal and organizational challenges
  • (as part of this panel, an exhibition of innovative startup projects by Ukrainian educators and researchers is also planned)
Main organizer: Innovation Ecosystem “Sikorsky Challenge Ukraine”.
Venue: Dragomanov Ukrainian State University.

4. The European vector of development for Ukrainian science and education

  • Harmonization of Ukrainian science and education with European standards
  • Adapting European practices for workforce training for the new economy
Main organizer: Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv.
Venue: Dragomanov Ukrainian State University.

5. Lifelong learning: economic, socio-labor and institutional challenges

  • Higher education within the framework of the new digital economy
  • Higher and platform-based education for the “silver economy”
  • Professional and social adaptation in veteran development policy
  • Strategies for the development of higher education in the context of “Industry 5.0” and “Work 5.0”
Main organizer: Vadym Hetman Kyiv National Economic University.
Venue: Dragomanov Ukrainian State University.

6. Training a new generation of teachers and educators

  • Competencies of the modern teacher and educator: innovation, critical thinking, digital literacy
  • Education as a tool for shaping national identity and restoring social unity
Main organizer: Mykhailo Drahomanov Ukrainian State University.
Venue: Dragomanov Ukrainian State University.

7. The role of youth in the development of science and education

  • Youth participation in reintegration processes
  • The challenge of bringing young people back to Ukraine
Main organizer: Youth Wing of the Ukrainian Peace Council.
Venue: Dragomanov Ukrainian State University.

8. Participation of international organizations in the reform of education in Ukraine

Main organizer: Media Center of the General Directorate for Servicing Foreign Representations (GDFR).
Venue: Media Center of the General Directorate for Servicing Foreign Representations (GDFR).

SECOND DAY:
Closing Plenary Session presenting panel results, discussing proposals, and adopting the Forum Resolution.
